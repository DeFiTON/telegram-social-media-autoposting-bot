# Telegram Social Media Auto-Posting Bot

> Forward a post to the bot — it automatically schedules and cross-posts your content across multiple social networks. Perfect for crypto creators, SMM managers and busy founders.

[![Telegram](https://img.shields.io/badge/Telegram-Open%20Bot-2CA5E0?logo=telegram&logoColor=white)](https://t.me/gknqkeiqdjqisdjiqfebot)

## What it is

This is a **Telegram bot for automated social media posting**. Instead of manually publishing the same content to every platform, you simply forward a post to the bot and it queues it for publication at a randomized future time. Drop in 50 posts today and your content calendar is filled for weeks ahead.

It is built for **content creators, crypto projects, SMM specialists and solo founders** who want a hands-off cross-posting workflow without paying for a heavyweight enterprise scheduler.

## Key features

- **Multi-platform cross-posting** — publish to X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Threads, Facebook and any other channel connected through your scheduling provider.
- **Crypto-friendly publishing** — a dedicated queue for crypto-focused social content with its own randomized scheduler.
- **Forward-to-publish workflow** — just forward a Telegram post (text, photo, photo + caption, or a multi-photo album) and the bot handles the rest.
- **Randomized scheduling** — every post is assigned a random publish time from 1 hour up to 10 days ahead, so your feed looks natural and consistent.
- **Carousel / album support** — a Telegram album of several photos is grouped into a single multi-image post.
- **Duplicate protection** — the bot fingerprints post text and blocks accidental re-posting of identical content.
- **Channel management** — turn individual destination channels on or off with inline buttons.
- **Queue visibility** — check how many posts are waiting and preview the next scheduled items.

## How to use

1. Open the bot: [@gknqkeiqdjqisdjiqfebot](https://t.me/gknqkeiqdjqisdjiqfebot)
2. Connect your destination channels in your social scheduling provider.
3. In the bot, run `/channels` and enable the channels you want to publish to.
4. **Forward any post** to the bot — it is automatically scheduled.
5. Use `/queue` to see how many posts are pending.

### Commands

| Command | Description |
|---|---|
| `/start` | Bot status, active channels, content queue |
| `/channels` | Enable / disable destination channels |
| `/queue` | Number of posts waiting in each queue |
| `/binance` | Preview the next scheduled crypto posts with times |
